Why Ceramic Tint?

Not All Tint Is Created Equal

Most budget window tint is dyed film — it darkens your windows but does little to block heat. It also fades to purple and bubbles over time. We exclusively install ceramic window film, which uses nano-ceramic particles to block infrared heat without interfering with electronics or fading over time.

Georgia summers are brutal. The right tint makes a real difference in cabin temperature, fuel efficiency, and interior preservation.

Common Questions

Window Tint FAQs

Georgia law requires front side windows to allow 32% or more light transmission. Rear side windows and the rear window can be any shade. The windshield can have a non-reflective tint strip along the top. We'll help you choose compliant shades during your consultation.

Most vehicles are done in 2–4 hours. Larger vehicles with more windows (SUVs, vans) may take 4–6 hours. We'll give you a time estimate when you book.

We recommend waiting at least 3–4 days before rolling windows down. This allows the adhesive to fully cure. Rolling windows down too early can cause the film to lift or peel at the edges.

This is normal during the first 1–2 weeks. Small water pockets and haziness appear during the curing process and will clear on their own. If bubbles remain after 3–4 weeks, contact us — we'll make it right.
