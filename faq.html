— Paint Protection Film

For most car owners — yes. PPF preserves your paint's condition, which directly protects your vehicle's resale value. A full-front install can pay for itself the first time a rock chip doesn't ruin your hood. On a $60,000+ vehicle, the math is easy. On a $20,000 car it depends on how long you're keeping it and how much your paint matters to you.

PPF is an investment — full-body installs start in the thousands. Film edges are visible if you look closely, especially on certain panel shapes. PPF won't fix existing paint defects — it protects going forward. And while it's removable, removal must be done professionally to avoid paint damage, especially on older or repainted panels.

Quality PPF is warrantied for 10 years against yellowing, cracking, blistering, and delamination. In practice, properly maintained film often lasts beyond 10 years. Heat, chemical exposure, and improper washing can shorten film life.

Partial front: ~1 day. Full front: 1–2 days. Track package: 2–3 days. Full body: 3–5 days. We'll give you a specific estimate based on your vehicle when you book.

Professionally removed PPF generally does not damage factory paint. However, on repainted panels, panels with existing paint damage, or very old vehicles, there can be risk. We assess paint condition before installation and flag any concerns upfront.

Wait 7 days before washing. After that: hand wash with pH-neutral soap, avoid automated car washes with brushes, and don't pressure-wash directly at film edges. We provide a full aftercare sheet at pickup.

— Ceramic Coating

Depending on the product tier and how the vehicle is maintained: entry-level coatings last 1–2 years, mid-tier 3–5 years, and flagship products 5–7+ years. Proper washing technique is the single biggest factor in how long a coating lasts.

No — ceramic coating adds scratch resistance against light surface swirls and chemical resistance, but it will not stop a rock chip. For rock chip and road debris protection, you need PPF. The two products complement each other well: PPF on high-impact zones, ceramic over the rest of the car.

Ideally, yes. Ceramic coating locks in your paint's current condition — swirl marks and scratches will still be visible under the coating. We recommend paint correction before any ceramic application for the best possible result. Ask about our paint correction add-on.

Wait at least 7–14 days before washing. Avoid water contact, bird droppings, and tree sap during the cure window. We'll give you specific guidance based on the product applied.

— Window Tint

Georgia requires front side windows to allow 32% or more light transmission (VLT). Rear side windows and the rear window can be any shade. A non-reflective tint strip is allowed on the top of the windshield. We'll help you choose compliant options during your consultation.

Regular dyed tint primarily blocks light. Ceramic tint uses nano-ceramic particles to block infrared heat without interfering with electronics. It rejects significantly more heat, won't fade or turn purple, and has no metallic content — so it doesn't interfere with GPS, phone signals, or key fobs. We exclusively install ceramic film.

Most vehicles: 2–4 hours. Larger vehicles (SUVs, vans with many windows): 4–6 hours. We'll give you a specific estimate when you book.

Wait at least 3–4 days before rolling windows down. Rolling them too early can cause the film to lift at the edges. During cure, you may notice slight haziness or small water pockets — this is normal and will clear on its own.
